Drought Intensity Classification For SDCI

  • Extreme Drought - The most intense drought level with widespread crop failures, severe water shortages, and emergency conditions requiring immediate intervention.
  • Severe Drought - Significant crop/pasture losses, frequent water shortages, and mandatory water use restrictions typically implemented.
  • Moderate Drought - Some damage to crops and pastures, low stream/river flows, and voluntary water conservation measures recommended.
  • Light Drought - Emerging dry conditions with potential stress on vegetation; early stages that may develop into more severe drought if conditions persist.
  • No Drought - Normal conditions with adequate water supply and healthy vegetation growth; baseline for comparison.
